### Core Thesis in One Sentence
> Transits are not good or bad in themselves but represent qualitatively different combinations of planetary influences that signify when specific life issues will arise, with their ease or difficulty determined by one's inner makeup rather than external fate.

## L1 - Deep Comprehension Layer

### L1.1 Source Text and Context

**Source Text**:
> "Traditionally there were only two kinds of transits, good ones and bad ones. The bad ones made everything go wrong, as if you were a helpless tool of malevolent fate. The good ones made everything go smoothly and ensured the success of any enterprise begun under their influence... But in modern astrology this notion has largely been abandoned. In theory at least, all transits are strictly neutral and merely represent qualitatively different combinations of the planetary influences of the natal and transiting planets."
>
> "I repeat this point because it cannot be stressed enough: the transits in themselves do not cause either good or bad happenings. In fact they do not cause anything; they are just signs that you will have to confront certain issues in your life at a particular time. The ease or difficulty of a particular transit comes from your inner makeup."

**Context Analysis**:

| Dimension | Analysis |
|:---|:---|

| Following direction | The text develops three interpretive models (harmonics, dynamic cycle, house approach) and provides detailed delineations of aspects |
| Overall positioning | Foundation chapter that establishes the epistemological shift from fatalistic to psychological astrology, setting the interpretive lens for all subsequent transit delineations |
