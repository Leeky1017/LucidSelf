### Core Thesis in One Sentence
> Mars represents the fundamental ego energy that maintains individuality against environmental pressure, traditionally called "malefic" but actually vital for personality; its negative manifestations (conflict, accidents, illness) arise primarily from suppression or excess rather than from the energy itself.

## L1 - Deep Comprehension Layer

### L1.1 Source Text and Context

**Source Text**:
> "Mars has traditionally been considered a malefic planet in astrology, that is, more likely to produce evil than good. But this is not so, although as is the case with any planet, if the energies of Mars are allowed to operate to excess, its effects can be quite difficult. But usually there is nothing to fear from its transits, and in fact its energies are a vital part of life."
>
> "Mars is an energy planet. Specifically it rules the energy that an individual uses to maintain himself in the face of pressures from his environment. The Mars energy says, 'This is what I am and I can be no other way!' and prepares the individual to fight, if necessary, to maintain his position. Obviously one can have no individuality without this energy."
>
> "The negative side of Mars most often manifests itself when there is no other outlet for its energies."
>
> "The only real danger with Mars is that its energies, when sublimated to the physical level, can signify accidents, specifically from burns or fire, and illnesses involving fever and infection. This is often the result of frustrated ego energies."
