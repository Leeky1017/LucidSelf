### Core Thesis in One Sentence
> Dream formation involves massive condensation: many latent thoughts are compressed into few manifest elements, so the remembered dream is laconic compared to its underlying thought material.

### L1.1 Source Text and Context

**Source Text** (selected lines within the semantic unit):
> (a) The Condensation Work  
> … a tremendous work of condensation has taken place.  
> The dream is reserved, paltry, and laconic when compared  
> with the range and copiousness of the dream thoughts. …  
> Thus the amount of  
> condensation is—strictly speaking—indeterminable. …  
>
> … dream formation is based on a  
> process of condensation, stands indubitable. How, then, is  
> this condensation brought about? …  
>
> Dream content: I have written a monograph upon a  
> (obscure) certain plant. The book lies before me, I am just  
> turning over a folded coloured plate. A dried specimen of the  
> plant is bound with every copy as though from a herbarium. …  
> the “botanical monograph” of  
> the dream turns out to be a common mean between the two  
> experiences of the day, and … bound up with the psychologically  
> significant experience by means of the most abundant  
> associations.
