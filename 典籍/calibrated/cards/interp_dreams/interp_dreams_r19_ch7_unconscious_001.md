### Core Thesis in One Sentence
> Dreams are wish-fulfilments because they are products of the unconscious system (Unc.), whereas symptoms reveal compromise formations involving both unconscious and foreconscious (Forec.) wish-streams.

### L1.1 Source Text and Context

**Source Text**:
> … we need no longer  
> delay entering upon the psychological investigations for which  
> we have so long prepared. …  
> We have  
> still, however, ample reason for giving further consideration  
> to the wish as the sole psychic motive power in the dream.  
> We have explained that the reason why the dream is in every  
> case a wish realisation is because it is a product of the Unc.,  
> which knows no other aim in its activity but the fulfilment  
> of wishes, and which has no other forces at its disposal but  
> wish-feelings. …  
>
> … the theory  
> of all psychoneurotic symptoms culminates in the proposition  
> that they too must be taken as wish-fulfilments of the unconscious.  
> … the formation of an hysterical symptom  
> necessitates the combination of both streams of our psychic  
> life. The symptom is not merely the expression of a realised  
> unconscious wish, but it must be joined by another wish from  
> the foreconscious which is fulfilled by the same symptom;  
> so that the symptom is at least doubly determined, once by  
> each one of the conflicting systems. …

### L1.2 Historical Background and Academic Lineage

| Dimension | Content |
|:---|:---|

| Academic lineage | Introduces system terminology (Unc., Forec.) that later becomes central to psychoanalytic metapsychology. |
| Later developments | Later psychology reworked or rejected topographic system models; nevertheless, the idea of competing motivational systems remains influential in various forms. |

### L1.3 Author's Intent and Rhetorical Analysis

| Dimension | Analysis |
|:---|:---|
| Author's intent | Justify universality of wish-fulfilment in dreams by anchoring it in a system (Unc.) defined by wish-aims, and connect to clinical symptoms. |
| Rhetorical strategy | Uses analogy/class inclusion: dreams are the “first member” of a group that includes symptoms; introduces double determination to explain differences. |
| Implicit assumptions | The Unc. has a single aim (wish fulfilment); system boundaries are meaningful explanatory units. |
