### Core Thesis in One Sentence
> Aspects are geometric angles between planets that link separate "bits" into integrated patterns—conjunction fuses functions inseparably, opposition creates productive tension requiring conscious balance, square generates friction that promotes growth through pressure, trine provides harmony with risk of laziness, and sextile produces mutual excitation—all reframed from simplistic "good/bad" to functional developmental terms.

### L1.1 Source Text and Context

**Source Text**:
> If signs, planets, and houses are the basic words in the astrological language, then aspects are the laws of grammar and syntax that govern how those words must be hooked together. [...] Many words exist in isolation. But to build complete sentences we must hook them together—and aspects are the hooks.
> Zero Degrees: The Conjunction [...] Fusion—that is the essence of the conjunction. Two "bits" become one. Neither planet can be activated without the other. [...] Conjunctions can be difficult or easy, depending on what planets are involved. The conjunction, in other words, is neither a "good" aspect nor a "bad" one.
> 180 Degrees: The Opposition [...] Tension—that is the key to understanding oppositions. [...] A man and a woman find themselves in a tempestuous, passionate marriage. [...] It is tense, but it is life. It is growth. [...] Classically, the opposition is considered to be a "bad" aspect. That is only half the truth.
> 90 Degrees: The Square [...] Squares produce friction [...] If the opposition is the aspect of rivals, then the square is the aspect of natural enemies. [...] There is no peace for a man with a tight square in his chart. But there is often greatness.
> 120 Degrees: The Trine [...] Trines mean harmony [...] Internal harmony—that is at once the great strength and the fatal weakness of trines. [...] Without the fire of squares and oppositions to keep our evolutionary flames burning, we go to sleep.
> 60 Degrees: The Sextile [...] Sextiles produce excitation. They are intense, colorful, dynamic. [...] Not so much the passionate marriage of the opposition. More a friendship [...] stimulating and exciting, but potentially a little giddy and unstable.

### L1.2 Historical Background and Academic Lineage

| Dimension | Content |
|:---|:---|
| Writing context | 1984/1988; Forrest writes against the traditional "benefic/malefic" aspect categorization that dominated popular astrology; part of the humanistic astrology movement's effort to reframe all symbols developmentally |
| Academic lineage | Aspect theory derives from Ptolemy (Tetrabiblos) who established the major aspects based on geometric relationships and classified them by difficulty; the five major aspects (conjunction, opposition, square, trine, sextile) are traditional Ptolemaic aspects; Forrest's functional reframing follows Dane Rudhyar's humanistic approach and anticipates later psychological astrology |
| Later developments | The "challenging aspects promote growth, easy aspects risk complacency" view became standard in psychological astrology education; this reframing influenced how modern astrologers counsel clients about difficult chart configurations; the "passionate marriage vs. friendly marriage" metaphor became widely quoted |

### L1.3 Author's Intent and Rhetorical Analysis

| Dimension | Analysis |
|:---|:---|
| Author's intent | Forrest wants to liberate readers from the fear of "bad" aspects and the complacency of "good" ones; he aims to show how all aspects serve development when understood functionally |
| Rhetorical strategy | Uses vivid relationship metaphors (passionate vs. friendly marriage) to make abstract geometry emotionally comprehensible; explicitly rejects traditional "good/bad" labels while acknowledging the grain of truth in them; presents each aspect with its developmental potential AND its risks; consistent format (degree, name, key quality, marriage metaphor, developmental implications) |
| Implicit assumptions | Assumes challenging aspects can serve growth if consciousness is applied; assumes harmony without challenge leads to stagnation; assumes the reader values growth over comfort |

### L2.3 Limitations and Applicability Boundaries

| Dimension | Content |
|:---|:---|
| Applicable conditions | Excellent for psychological interpretation and counseling; promotes nuanced understanding; helps clients reframe "difficult" charts positively; works well for natal interpretation focused on growth |
| Inapplicable conditions | Traditional horary astrology may require benefic/malefic judgments for prediction; electional astrology uses traditional aspect classifications; some mundane applications need predictive rather than developmental framing |

### L3.2 Abstract Rule Extraction

**Rule ID**: `rule_inner_sky_aspect_interpretation`

**Rule Structure** (pseudo-code form):

```
FUNCTION interpret_aspect(planet_A, planet_B, aspect_type, orb):
  # Identify the functions involved
  function_A = A.psychological_function
  function_B = B.psychological_function
  
  # Apply aspect-specific interpretation
  IF aspect_type == "conjunction" (0° ± orb):
    relationship = "fusion"
    description = "{A} and {B} are inseparable; one activates the other"
    quality = "neither good nor bad; depends on planets"
    developmental_task = "integrate fused functions consciously"
    
  IF aspect_type == "opposition" (180° ± orb):
    relationship = "creative_tension"
    description = "{A} and {B} are in productive tension"
    quality = "challenging but potentially balancing"
    developmental_task = "hold both poles consciously; find synthesis"
    
  IF aspect_type == "square" (90° ± orb):
    relationship = "friction"
    description = "{A} and {B} are natural enemies; neither can rest"
    quality = "challenging; promotes growth through pressure"
    developmental_task = "use friction as fuel for achievement"
    
  IF aspect_type == "trine" (120° ± orb):
    relationship = "harmony"
    description = "{A} and {B} cooperate easily"
    quality = "easy but risks laziness"
    developmental_task = "actively use the gift; don't take it for granted"
    
  IF aspect_type == "sextile" (60° ± orb):
    relationship = "excitation"
    description = "{A} and {B} stimulate each other"
    quality = "dynamic but potentially unstable"
    developmental_task = "channel excitation productively"
  
  RETURN interpretation(relationship, description, quality, developmental_task)

WITH confidence = High
BECAUSE "aspects are the laws of grammar and syntax that govern how those words must be hooked together"
UNLESS context == horary OR context == electional (different rules may apply)
```

**Rule Operationalization**:

| Dimension | Explanation |

| Conclusion operationalization | Describe how the two planetary functions interact: what is the relationship type, what is the developmental task, what are the risks and opportunities |
| Confidence rationale | High; aspect interpretation is fundamental to astrology; Forrest's functional framing is well-established in psychological astrology |
